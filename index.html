Philippe Desjardins-Proulx

I'm a computational scientist working on machine learning (a branch of Artificial Intelligence) and its application to ecology & evolution. I'm working on inductive transfer, which in layman's terms could be described as the capacity to transfer knowledge from one task to another. Humans are expert at this, we almost effortlessly exploit the knowledge acquired from our past experiences when we encounter new challenges. We are good at knowing what kind of knowledge can be transferred, for example some concepts in economy are similar to concepts in population ecology: competition, limits to growth, while other important aspects of economy, such as labor laws, have little to do with ecology.

Modern machine learning algorithms tend to learn a model from data without exploiting prior knowledge. This method has advantages but makes it hard to build strong models when little data is available or when the context has suddenly changed. Also, I think the problem of inductive transfer will force us to adopt more sophisticated representations of data, and thus move us closer to true intelligence.

I'm doing a Ph.D. at the Theoretical Ecosystem Ecology laboratory and the Quebec Center for Biodiversity Science. My work is supported by an Alexander Graham Bell Graduate Scholarship from the NSERC. You can get my short CV here.
