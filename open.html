Short personal statements on open access in scientific publications and open source software.

Open Access

Science is a collective project and I cannot see how, in the era of the internet, we can justify hiding our (often publicly funded) research output. Scientific papers should be available before being submitted to journals for peer-review. I always submit my first-author manuscripts to arXiv or a similar service (e.g.: github, bitbucket, FigShare ) before submitting them to a journal. Also, I always either store the final PDF on my website or, in the case of open access publications, link to the PDF. I cannot submit the manuscript to arXiv when I'm not the first author, though I certainly hope the first author will do so, but the final PDF will always be accessible on my website.

Open Source

I'm comfortable with the full range of licenses: from the liberal MIT to the more restrictive GPL and even proprietary software . In practice, I think the basic infrastructure on which we build software has to be open source. It's the only way to ensure that no single corporation is in a position to impose its will on software development. Thus, I strongly favor open source operating systems, browsers, databases, compilers, interpreters. When it comes to more specialized software: the more liberal the better (MIT, Apache), copyleft if necessary (it did serve Linux well), but I also understand the need to keep the code private and adopt a proprietary license.
